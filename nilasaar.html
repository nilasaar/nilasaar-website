The Origin

Araku
doesn't rush.

Deep in the Eastern Ghats, where the valleys stay cool and the air carries no exhaust, wildflowers bloom without instruction. No pesticides. No schedules. No interference.

The bees here have been doing the same thing for centuries — following the same flowers, building the same combs, producing honey that tastes like the forest it came from. Complex, raw, and completely alive.

This is where Nilasaar begins.

The Story

I wasn't looking
to start a business.

I grew up eating sugar in every form it came. When I finally started paying attention to what I was putting in my body, honey felt like a safe choice — until I started reading labels.

What I found on those shelves didn't feel like honey. It felt like a performance of honey.

So I went back to Araku — a valley I'd loved since childhood — and found what I'd been looking for. Honey that tasted like nowhere else. Because it comes from nowhere else.

I couldn't keep that to myself.

— Founder, Nilasaar
